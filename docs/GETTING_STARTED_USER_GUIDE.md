## Part 1: Installation (5 minutes)

### Step 1: Download Nakamoto

1. Visit: **https://nakamoto.network/download**
2. Click the big blue button: **"Download for Windows"**
3. Save the file (`NakamotoSetup.exe`) to your Downloads folder

**File size**: About 45 MB (same as 10 songs)

### Step 2: Install

1. **Find** the file you downloaded (usually in Downloads folder)
2. **Double-click** `NakamotoSetup.exe`
3. If Windows asks "Do you want to allow this app?", click **"Yes"**
4. Click **"Next"** a few times (accept the defaults)
5. Click **"Install"**
6. Wait 2-3 minutes ☕
7. Click **"Finish"**

**Done!** You'll see a Nakamoto icon on your desktop.

### What Just Happened?

You installed:
- **Nakamoto Browser** - For browsing blockchain websites
- **Nakamoto Node** - Your connection to the blockchain network

Think of it like installing Chrome (browser) + the internet connection (node).

## Part 2: First Launch (3 minutes)

### Step 1: Open Nakamoto

**Double-click** the Nakamoto icon on your desktop.

You'll see a welcome screen:
```
Welcome to Nakamoto!
Let's set up your first wallet...
```

### Step 2: Create Your Wallet

**What's a wallet?**  
Think of it like a digital bank account, but **you're** the bank. No one can freeze it, block it, or charge fees without your permission.

1. The browser will ask: **"Wallet Name"**
   - Type something like: `My Main Wallet`
2. Click **"Create Wallet"**

### Step 3: SUPER IMPORTANT - Save Your Seed Phrase! ⚠️

You'll see 12 words like:
```
apple forest mountain river cloud sunset ...
```

**This is CRITICAL:**
- These 12 words are the **ONLY** way to recover your wallet
- If you lose your computer, you'll need these words
- **Write them on paper** (yes, actual paper!)
- **Never** type them into any website or app
- **Never** take a screenshot (could be hacked)

**Where to store them**:
- ✅ Written on paper in a safe place
- ✅ In a fireproof safe
- ✅ Given to a trusted family member
- ❌ On your computer
- ❌ In the cloud (Dropbox, Google Drive, etc.)
- ❌ Emailed to yourself

**Done?** Check the box that says "I have written down my seed phrase" and click **"Continue"**.

### Step 4: Verify Your Seed Phrase

To make sure you wrote it correctly:
- The browser will ask for word #3, word #7, etc.
- Look at your paper and type them
- Click **"Verify"**

### Step 5: Set a Password

Create a password to protect your wallet on **this computer**:
- At least 8 characters
- Mix of letters, numbers, symbols
- Something you'll remember!

**Click "Finish Setup"**

🎉 **Congratulations!** You have a Nakamoto wallet!

## Part 4: Getting Your First NAK (5 minutes)

You have a wallet, but it's empty! Let's get some NAK.

### Option 1: Ask a Friend

The easiest way!

**Your Address** (like a bank account number):
1. Click **💰 Wallet** tab
2. Click **"Receive"**
3. You'll see your address: `nak1qsxxxxxxx...`
4. Click **"Copy Address"**
5. Send this to your friend (via text, email, etc.)

**When they send NAK**:
- You'll see it arrive in 30-60 seconds
- First transaction might take longer

### Option 2: Convert Bitcoin

If you have Bitcoin (BTC):

1. Click **💰 Wallet** → **Bitcoin**
2. Click **"Generate BTC Address"**
3. Send Bitcoin to that address
4. Wait for 6 confirmations (about 60 minutes)
5. Your NAK balance will automatically increase!

**Conversion rate**: 1 satoshi (smallest Bitcoin unit) = 1 NAK

### Option 3: Buy on Exchange

(Coming soon - exchanges are adding NAK support)

## Part 5: Sending NAK (3 minutes)

### Send Your First Transaction

1. Click **💰 Wallet** tab
2. Click **"Send"**
3. Fill in the form:
   ```
   To:      [paste recipient's nak1q... address]
   Amount:  [100]  (try a small amount first!)
   Fee:     [10]   (recommended: 10-50 NAK)
   ```
4. Click **"Send"**
5. Enter your password
6. Click **"Confirm"**

**What happens next?**
- Transaction is broadcast to the network
- Appears as "Pending" for 30-60 seconds
- Changes to "Confirmed" when complete
- NAK is removed from your balance

**Important**:
- Transactions **cannot** be reversed!
- Always double-check the recipient address
- Test with small amounts first

## Part 6: Browsing the Blockchain (2 minutes)

### Visit Your First Blockchain Website

**What are blockchain websites?**  
Regular websites (google.com, facebook.com) are stored on servers that companies control. Blockchain websites are stored on the blockchain - decentralized and uncensorable!

**Try these**:

1. Click in the address bar (top)
2. Type: `nak://marketplace`
3. Press Enter

**Or try**:
- `nak://news` - Blockchain news
- `nak://exchange` - Exchange NAK for other currencies
- `nak://help` - Help center

**How is this different?**
- No one can take these sites offline
- No tracking or ads (unless the site chooses)
- Paid for with NAK instead of traditional ads

### Bookmark Your Favorites

Found a site you like?
1. Click the ⭐ star icon
2. It's saved to your bookmarks!

Access bookmarks: Click [☰] menu → **Bookmarks**

## Part 7: Storing a File (3 minutes)

### Upload to the Blockchain

**Why?**  
Files on the blockchain are:
- 🔒 Permanent (can't be deleted)
- 🌍 Accessible anywhere
- 🚫 Uncensorable
- 👤 Controlled by you

**Let's try it**:

1. Click **📁 Files** tab
2. Click **"Upload File"**
3. Choose a file from your computer (start small - under 1 MB)
4. You'll see:
   ```
   File: photo.jpg
   Size: 500 KB
   Cost: 5 NAK
   
   ☑ Make public (anyone can download)
   ☐ Keep private (only you)
   ```
5. Choose public or private
6. Click **"Upload"**
7. Wait a few seconds
8. **Done!** Your file is on the blockchain forever!

**You'll get a link like**:
```
nak://QmYwAPJzv5CZsnA625s3Xf2nemtYgPpHdWEz79ojWnPbdG
```

Share this link with anyone! They can download your file in their Nakamoto browser.

**Cost**: 10 NAK per MB (one-time, stored forever!)

## Common Questions

### How do I get more NAK?

- **Receive from friends** (free)
- **Convert Bitcoin** (1:1 satoshi ratio)
- **Buy on exchange** (once available)
- **Become a validator** (advanced - earn by securing network)

### Is my money safe?

**Yes, if you**:
- ✅ Saved your seed phrase (on paper!)
- ✅ Use a strong password
- ✅ Never share your seed phrase
- ✅ Keep your computer updated

**Your NAK cannot be**:
- ❌ Frozen by a bank
- ❌ Seized by authorities (without your seed phrase)
- ❌ Hacked (if you follow security best practices)

### What if I lose my computer?

**If you have your seed phrase** (those 12 words):
1. Install Nakamoto on new computer
2. Click "Recover Wallet"
3. Enter your 12 words
4. Your wallet is restored! ✅

**If you DON'T have your seed phrase**:
- Your wallet is lost forever ❌
- This is why we emphasized saving it!

### Can I undo a transaction?

**No!** Blockchain transactions are permanent.

Always:
- Double-check the address
- Start with small test amounts
- Verify before confirming

### How much does it cost to use Nakamoto?

- **Downloading the software**: Free
- **Creating a wallet**: Free
- **Receiving NAK**: Free
- **Sending NAK**: 10-50 NAK per transaction (like a postage stamp)
- **Storing files**: 10 NAK per MB (one-time payment)

### Is this legal?

Yes! Cryptocurrency is legal in most countries, though regulations vary.

**Check your local laws** regarding cryptocurrency ownership and trading.

### What's the difference between NAK and Bitcoin?

**Bitcoin (BTC)**:
- Digital gold
- Focused on being money
- Slower, higher fees

**Nakamoto (NAK)**:
- Digital internet
- Browsing websites + money + file storage
- Faster, lower fees
- 1:1 conversion with Bitcoin (1 satoshi = 1 NAK)

You can use both! Many people convert BTC → NAK to use blockchain features.

## Troubleshooting

### I forgot my password

**Solution**: Use your seed phrase to recover

1. Click "Forgot Password?" on login screen
2. Enter your 12-word seed phrase
3. Set a new password

### My balance shows 0 but I received NAK

**Wait a few minutes** - transactions need confirmation

**Check**:
1. Click **💰 Wallet** → **History**
2. Look for "Pending" transactions
3. Wait for "Confirmed" status

### The browser won't open

**Try**:
1. Right-click Nakamoto icon → "Run as administrator"
2. If still not working, restart your computer
3. Still stuck? Uninstall and reinstall

### I can't send a transaction - "Insufficient balance"

**Check**:
- Do you have enough NAK for amount + fee?
- Example: Sending 100 NAK needs 110 NAK total (100 + 10 fee)

**Wait for confirmations**:
- Recently received NAK needs 6 confirmations (≈60 seconds)

### File upload failed

**Check**:
- File size under 100 MB
- You have enough NAK (10 NAK per MB)
- Good internet connection

**Try**:
- Smaller file first
- Wait and try again

## Safety Tips

### Protect Your Seed Phrase

**DO**:
- ✅ Write it on paper
- ✅ Store in a safe place
- ✅ Make a backup copy (separate location)
- ✅ Tell a trusted person where it is (in case of emergency)

**DON'T**:
- ❌ Take a screenshot
- ❌ Email it to yourself
- ❌ Store in cloud (Dropbox, Google Drive, etc.)
- ❌ Share with anyone (even support!)
- ❌ Store on your computer

### Recognize Scams

**Warning signs**:
- 🚩 Someone asks for your seed phrase
- 🚩 "Double your NAK!" offers
- 🚩 Urgent messages demanding immediate action
- 🚩 Too good to be true promises

**Remember**:
- Support will **NEVER** ask for your seed phrase
- There are no "giveaways" requiring you to send NAK first
- Always verify website URLs

### Start Small

When trying something new:
- Send a small test transaction first
- Upload a small test file first
- Practice with amounts you can afford to lose

## Glossary

**Simple Terms**:

**Blockchain** - A shared record everyone can see but no one can change

**Cryptocurrency** - Digital money you control (like cash, but online)

**Wallet** - Where your cryptocurrency is stored (like a bank account you control)

**Seed Phrase** - 12 words that are the master key to your wallet

**NAK** - The cryptocurrency used in Nakamoto (like Bitcoin, but for Nakamoto)

**Transaction** - Sending NAK from one wallet to another

**Address** - Like a bank account number (starts with "nak1q...")

**Confirmation** - Verification that your transaction is complete

**Blockchain Website** - Website stored on blockchain instead of a server

**Content Hash** - Unique ID for files on the blockchain (starts with "Qm...")

**Fee** - Small amount paid to process transactions (like a postage stamp)

**Decentralized** - No single company or person in control
